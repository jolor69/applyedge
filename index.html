You got the interview

Now win it.

Getting the call is only half the battle. Six research-backed practices that separate candidates who get offers from those who don't.

01

Use the STAR Method

For every behavioural question, structure your answer as Situation → Task → Action → Result. Keep Situation and Task brief — get to your Action within 15 seconds. The interviewer wants to hear what you did and what happened.

Source: DDI (pioneers since 1974) · Indeed
02

Research Beyond the Job Post

Read the company's latest news, annual report, and LinkedIn posts. Know their competitors and market position. Interviewers notice — and reward — candidates who understand where the company is going, not just what it does today.

Source: Google Careers · Experis
03

Prepare 3–5 Selling Points

Before you walk in, know exactly what makes you the best candidate. Prepare three to five specific, evidence-backed selling points. Weave them into your answers throughout — don't wait to be asked directly.

Source: Experis — 20 Interview Tips
04

Master Virtual Eye Contact

68% of first-round interviews are now virtual. Look at the camera lens — not the screen — when delivering your key points. This creates direct eye contact and makes your answers land with far more authority.

Source: SHRM 2026 via The Interview Guys
05

Arrive Early — Always

For in-person: arrive 10–15 minutes early, map your route in advance. For virtual: test your camera, mic, and connection 30 minutes before. Technical failures at the start signal disorganisation and are very hard to recover from.

Source: Indeed — 21 Interview Tips
06

Ask Strategic Closing Questions

End strong. Ask: "If I joined, what would my first project be?" or "What does success look like after 90 days?" These create positive visualisation and signal you're already thinking about contributing.

Source: The Interview Guys — 24hr Framework
